Pad Thai

Description

Pad Thai is a dish typically made with rice noodles, shrimp, peanuts, egg, sugar, and bean sprouts. This dish's origin is easy to spot as it is in the name, Thailand! Even though stir-fried rice noodles arrived in Thailand from China centuries ago, Pad Thai was invented in the mid-20th century.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz package of rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tbsp white sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • ⅛ tbsp crushed red pepper
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • ¼ cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Soak rice noodles in bowl of cold water until they are soft, 30 to 50 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Meanwhile, heat butter inside a wok; add chicken and saut√© until browned. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  4. Heat oil in the wok over medium-high heat. Crack the eggs into hot oil and cook until firm. Stir the chicken in and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add softened noodles, sugar, fish sauce, vinegar, and red pepper; mix well until noodles are tender. Season to taste.
  6. Stir bean sprouts into wok and cook for 3 minutes.
  7. Serve topped with green onions, crushed peanuts, and a lemon wedge.
